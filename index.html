about

The dachshund is a short-legged, long-bodied, hound dog. The standard size dachshund was developed to scent, chase, and flush out badgers and other burrow-dwelling animals. They originate from Germany. They live to be 12-16 years old. The Dachshund might be the smallest of the hounds, but they are the biggest in spirit. Their distinctive shape first catches the eye, but people who know them appreciate their character, intelligence, hunting spirit, and absolute devotion to his people.
